About

Bertrand's Postulate was first described as a conjecture in 1845 by Joseph Bertrand, who later proved it for all numbers between 3 and 3,000,000. Because the statement was completely proved by Pafnuty Chebyschev in 1852, the postulate is also often referred to as the Bertrand-Chebyshev Theorem or Chebyshev's Theorem. The theorem states that for any integer x greater than 3, there exists at least one prime number between x and 2x-2.
