Bertrand's Postulate was first described as a conjecture in 1845 by Joseph Bertrand, who later proved it for all numbers between 3 and 3,000,000. Because the statement was completely proved by Pafnuty Chebyschev in 1852, the postulate is also often referred to as the Bertrand-Chebyshev Theorem or Chebyshev's Theorem. The theorem states that for any integer 'x' greater than 3, there exists at least one prime number between 'x' and 2x-2. This can also be expressed as x < p < 2x-2 for x > 3 and 'p' being a prime number.
