Bertrand's Postulate

Bertrand's Postulate was first described as a
conjecture in 1845 by Joseph Bertrand, who later
proved it for all numbers between and including 2
and 3,000,000. Because the statement was completely
proved by Pafnuty Chebyshev, in 1852, the postulate
is often also refered to as the "Bertrand-Chebyshev
Theorem" or "Chebyshev's Theorem." The theorem itself
states that for any integer "x" greater than 3, there
exists at least one prime number in between x and
2x-2. The code to the right first takes an integer
input and verifies that it is indeed an integer
greater than 3. If it is, then the program uses
the postulate to find and report all qualifying primes.
