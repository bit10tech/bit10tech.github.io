About

Also known as The 3n+1 Conjecture, Hasse's Algorithm, and The Thwaites Problem, The Collatz Conjecture observes an assumption regarding sequences of known positive integers. The conjecture states that if the previous term is even, the term that follows it is half the previous term. On the contrary, if the previous term is odd, the term that follows it is that term multiplied by 3 then added to 1. By the rules of this conjecture, the sequence will eventually terminate at 1, regardless of the initial number.
