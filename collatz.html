The Collatz Conjecture

The Collatz Conjecture - also known as the "3n+1
Conjecture," "Hasse's Algorithm," "Kakutani's Problem,"
"The Syracuse Problem," and "The Thwaites Problem" -
observes an assumption made regarding sequences of
known positive integers. The conjecture states that if
the previous term is even, the term that follows it is
half the previous term. On the contrary, if the previous
term is odd, the term that follows it is 3 times the
previous term plus 1. By the rules of this conjecture,
the sequence will eventually terminate at 1, regardless
of the initial number. The code to the right outlines
