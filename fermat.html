First proved by Euler in the 17th century, Fermat's Theorem on Sums of Square Numbers details an observation on certain prime numbers called Pythagorean Primes. Pythagorean Primes are prime numbers that can be summed by two square integers. For example, 5 is a Pythagorean Prime number as it is the sum of 1 squared (1) and 2 squared (4). The list of these numbers can be summed up as the list of primes that are 1 under modulo 4, and each prime 'p' can be expressed as p = x2 + y2 for 'x' and 'y' integers.
