Fermat's Theorem on Sums of Square Numbers

Fermat's Theorem on sums of square numbers -
first proved by Euler in the 17th century - details
an observation on certain prime numbers called
Pythagorean Primes. Pythagorean Primes are prime
numbers that can be summed by two squared integers.
For example, 5 is a Pythagorean Prime number as it
is the sum of 1 squared (1) and two squared (4). The
list of these numbers can be summed up as the list of
primes that are 1 under mod 4. The code to the right
first determines whether the number entered qualifies
(if it is a Pythagorean Prime) then calculates all
pairs of positive integers that, when squared, sum
to the entered number, if it is a Pythagorean Prime.
