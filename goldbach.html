About

One of the oldest unsolved problems in number theory, goldbach's Conjecture was first proposed in a letter to Leonhard Euler in 1742. Christian Goldbach's idea states that if an integer is even and greater than 2, then it can be expressed as the sum of two primes. For example, 6 can be expressed by 3+3 and 8 can be expressed as 3+5. Thus far, the conjecture has been proved for integers up to 4E18, though it has yet to be solved entirely.
