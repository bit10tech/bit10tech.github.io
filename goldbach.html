Goldbach's Conjecture

One of the oldest unsolved problems in number
theory, Goldbach's Conjecture was first proposed
in a letter to Leonhard Euler in 1742. Christian
Goldbach's idea states that if an integer is even
and greater than 2, then it can be expressed as the
sum of two primes. For example, 6 can be expressed
as 3+3 and 8 can be expressed as 3+5. Thus far, the
conjecture has been proved for integers up to 4E18,
however, it has yet to be solved entirely. The code
to the right first determines if the entered integer
is even and greater than 2. If it is, then it applies
Goldbach's Conjecture and reports all pairs of prime
numbers that sum to the specified integer.
