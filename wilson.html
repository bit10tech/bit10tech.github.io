Proved by Joseph-Louis Langrange in 1770, Wilson's Theorem was proposed by John Wilson in the 18th century to determine whether or not a positive integer is prime. The theorem states that if the factorial of a number minus 1 in the modulo of that number equals -1 in the modulo of that number, then the number is prime. for example, 2, a known prime number, satisfies this theorem because the factorial of 2-1 (1) is 1. In mod 2, -1 is equal to 1 and thus equal to the factorial of our originial number, 2, minus 1.
